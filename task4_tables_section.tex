\subsection{Baseline Evaluation: Results, Figures, and Tables}
\label{subsec:baseline_eval_results}

This subsection reports the key numeric summaries extracted from the baseline evaluation and the next-steps diagnostics. The analysis is designed to make trade-offs explicit (power, efficiency, over-provisioning), expose method collapse via diversity statistics, and summarize stability between the minimum and recommended tracks. These diagnostics follow standard sensitivity analysis practice \cite{Saltelli2008,Saltelli2010} and use rank correlation to assess ordering stability across tracks \cite{Spearman1904}.

\paragraph{Why this analysis matters.}
GPU selection is a constrained, multi-objective decision. Pure performance selection wastes power, while pure efficiency can under-provision. The analysis quantifies these tensions and shows where different objectives collapse to the same choice, which is critical for interpreting whether the proposed method offers meaningful trade-offs rather than trivial agreement.

\paragraph{Industry relevance.}
The results inform practical GPU selection for PC builders, OEM configuration, and energy-aware procurement. Metrics like PSU headroom and performance-per-watt map directly to operational cost, thermal limits, and sustainability targets in real deployments.

\paragraph{Interpretation of figures.}
Figures \ref{fig:avg_tdp}--\ref{fig:pareto} show that performance-first baselines dominate power usage, while efficiency baselines converge on low-power GPUs. Pareto and safety-factor baselines occupy the middle ground, which is the intended behavior for multi-objective selection.
